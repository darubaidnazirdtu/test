LOCATION·STAMP

Live capture only
Take a photo
Opens your camera · location + time are read live
Location and time are captured live from your device at the moment you take the photo — they are not editable, so the stamp reflects where and when the photo was actually taken. Everything happens in your browser; the photo itself is never uploaded. A location lookup (reverse geocoding) is sent to a public address-lookup service to turn coordinates into a readable address — only the coordinates leave your device, not the image.
